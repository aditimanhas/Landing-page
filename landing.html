PROGRAMME CURRICULUM

BITS Law School offers two five-year integrated honours programmes: the B.A. LL.B. (Hons.) and B.B.A. LL.B. (Hons.). These programmes feature an interdisciplinary curriculum that blends mooting, clinical and legal education, internships, and Readiness at Workplace (RAW) modules. This approach provides a robust foundation in the social sciences, arts, and humanities, business and economics, essential for mastering and excelling in the legal field.
The programmes aim to elevate legal education and prepare students for successful careers in their chosen legal specialisations. Additionally, they focus on fostering a creative, socially conscious, and ethical mindset, deeply rooted in social and cultural practices.

INTERNSHIP & MENTORSHIP PROGRAM

Internships are integral in building well-rounded legal professionals and enabling students to recognise and appreciate law 'in practice' and 'in action'. Our dedicated Industry Interface and Career Advancement (IICA) team is at the heart of BITS Law School's commitment to providing diverse and enriching internship experiences. The IICA Team facilitates, supports, and curates internships for law students. They connect with recruiters from various fields, including Corporates, Domestic and International Law Firms, The Judiciary, Legal Regulatory Authorities, Management Consulting Firms, Legislatures, Parliamentarians, Appellate Advocates, ADR and Mediation Firms, Start-ups, Research Institutions, Think Tanks, and NGOs.
The academic core curriculum is supplemented with our Readiness at the Workplace (RAW) programme that has been instrumental in offering students invaluable real-world insights through interactive sessions and skill enhancement workshops led by industry experts.
BITS Law School's Founding Class of 120 students has secured 100% internship in the academic year 2023-2024.

ELIGIBILITY

    Our admissions process is carefully designed to holistically evaluate each applicant, giving them every opportunity to demonstrate their strengths and abilities. Eligibility and evaluation parameters are as follows:

  • Applicant must have a minimum of 45% marks in Grade 12 or equivalent from a recognised board and a valid entrance exam score to participate in the admission process. This however does not guarantee selection to BITS Law School, which follows a holistic process based on a complete application form, marks in Grade 12, co/extra-curricular activities, personal interview, and an entrance exam score.
  • Law entrance examination scores accepted - BITS Law Admission Test/CLAT/LNAT/AILET/MHCET Law.
  • Co-curricular achievements.
  • Written and oral communication skills.
  • Applicants yet to receive their final grade 12 and/or law entrance exam scores are eligible to apply and will be expected to indicate in their application form that they are awaiting their results.
